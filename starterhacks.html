Overview

StarterHacks is a beginner-focused hackathon, a weekend-long tech competition that allows students to work together to build a product and pitch their ideas to judges. We focus on promoting inclusivity, collaboration, and education, and make efforts to welcome students of all backgrounds to experience the tech industry.





Design Director (May 2018 to May 2019)

One of my first tasks as Design Director at StarterHacks was to envision the branding for the 2019 season of the event to reflect our values, and refresh the website experience to meet our objectives. As the lead, I managed the team of 5 to meet deadlines for deliverables and make design decisions that would present the best image for our brand.





The Challenge


How might we engage stakeholders and attract interest in our event, and provide a welcoming physical and digital experience?





Our Brand



Responsive & Accessible Logos

Knowing that the StarterHacks logo would be used on various digital channels, our logo was designed by last year’s design team to be responsive, and be adaptable to fit more than just a horizontal space. This would allow for the logo to scale to a mobile format later on. Black and white variations of the logo were provided to keep the logo visible depending on the surface it would be placed on.




Colour Palette

StarterHacks has 3 main colours, representing the streams of hackers at our event: Engineering (pink), Design (blue), and Business (green). Yellow is our accent colour. Taking into account our heavy use of gradients in our vector style, we also included light and dark variations of our main colour to be used together to create gradients. The light and dark shades also make our design more accessible.

Adding Delightful Illustrations

Our website design strongly emphasized using vector illustrations. We incorporated our vibrant colour palette to make the illustrations fun, and included plant motifs to represent the growth and learning that our hackathon provides to first-time hackers. Final Designs & Outcomes


Making navigation efficient with a sticky(ish) header


Another design choice my team and I made was to set the website as one continuous scroll. This was decided for multiple reasons:

1. Decrease the number of clicks necessary to navigate our site.

2. Guide the user through the page using layout and vertical format, with call-to-action buttons in between.



Nurturing trust in our event with attendee testimonials

With hacker engagement in mind, my team and I decided to feature the experiences of past hackers to appeal to potential new hackers. We wanted to present the StarterHacks experience through the perspective of someone who was there in person.
