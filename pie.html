PIE - Blockchain Charity Platform


Role

User Experience Designer

Hackathon

Hack the North 2018

Duration

36 Hours


Brief

PIE is a platform that uses the capabilities of blockchain in order to make secure cryptocurrency-based donations to charities. The platform keeps charities accountable for their transactions through ledgers. We wanted to empower users on the platform to donate to causes they care about, with the ability to vote on proposals or causes that matter to them.



User Story

As a: Charity Donor
I want to: track what my donation is being used towards
So that: I can confirm that my money is used towards the cause I am passionate about and keep charities accountable


Project Goals


How might we:

Demystify cyptocurrency and make the donation flow easy to understand

Organize and present data to user to aleviate their concerns





Wireframes

After defining our problem and user story, I got right into wireframing. My challenge in the beginning was mostly understanding how blockchain functioned. Coming from a non-technical background, it was hard for me to make informed design decisions without firstly understanding the core of our product.

Once that was understood, I eagerly moved on to creating wireframes!










Final Designs & Outcomes


Dashboard


The dashboard is the homepage a user lands on after signing in. In contains data from all the charities they support and how much money they are raising. There is basic info about their balances like with most financial applications, with CTAs to donate.



Donation Flow


The donation flow is simple, and places a focus on the charity option rather than the blockchain. In order to make the experience more welcoming, we wanted the fact that the platform was based in crypto to be a lesser detail. The main purpose after all, is making charitable donations.



Charity Profile


From the front page or after donation, you can view a charity's profile to track their spend history. This way, users have more transparency into where their money is going, and therefore increasing trust between the organization and customer.









Takeaways


I really learned a lot about blockchain in this weekend! From knowing nothing to being able to explain it in general terms to a beginner is great in my book. The challenge here was really designing something I had little knowledge about. I had to go off a lot of assumptions, especially with the time restraints of a hackathon. It has definitely made me appreciate UX designers in more technical industries, which would require knowledge in those fields as well.
